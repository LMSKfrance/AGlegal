###### Key impact of immigration policies on criminal defense

Recent shifts in immigration policies have had profound effects on criminal defense strategies. Defense attorneys are increasingly faced with the challenge of navigating both criminal law and immigration law to ensure that their clients' rights are upheld in the face of legal complexities.

###### The intersection of criminal and immigration law

Immigration status can significantly influence the outcome of criminal cases, particularly for non-citizens. Legal experts are advising that attorneys stay informed about immigration policies to better advocate for their clients, especially when it comes to sentencing and deportation risks.

##### How criminal defense attorneys are adapting

Criminal defense attorneys must now be well-versed in both criminal and immigration law. This dual expertise allows them to craft strategies that protect their clients from the full impact of criminal charges, especially when immigration consequences are at stake.

![Immigration Policy Changes and Criminal Defense](/images/ag-legal-about.jpg)

###### The role of recent immigration reforms

As immigration policies evolve, criminal defense lawyers must understand how new reforms, such as changes in deportation procedures or visa regulations, might affect their clients. Keeping up-to-date with these changes is critical in providing effective legal representation.

##### Protecting clients’ immigration status

In addition to defending clients against criminal charges, attorneys must consider the immigration consequences of a conviction. This includes exploring options for waivers, appeals, or alternative legal solutions to mitigate deportation risks.

The relationship between immigration and criminal law is increasingly important as more cases involve both areas of law. Criminal defense attorneys are encouraged to partner with immigration experts to ensure comprehensive protection for their clients, safeguarding both their criminal case and their immigration status.
